### The Reality Check
Today, I had a 30-minute technical interview. While I’ve maintained a **4.0 GPA at UTD**, this session reminded me that "knowing" a language and "performing" in a live environment are two different skills.

### What Went Well
* **The Architecture Discussion:** We dove into my **C++ Matching Engine**. Explaining the $O(1)$ complexity of my order book felt natural.
* **The Pivot:** Even when I tripped up on a specific Java Palindrome check, I was able to walk through my logic out loud.

### The "Gap" (What I'm Fixing)
I realized my knowledge of **Java Checked vs. Unchecked Exceptions** was a bit rusty. In a classroom, you handle them to pass the test; in production, you handle them to prevent a system-wide crash.

### My "Summer of Growth" Plan
Instead of letting one interview define my summer, I’m using it as a launchpad:
1.  **AWS Solutions Architect Certification:** Moving from local code to cloud-scale.
2.  **LeetCode Mastery:** Specifically focusing on the "Two Pointers" and "Sliding Window" patterns.
3.  **Open Source:** Contributing to Python/Java libraries to see how senior devs structure code.
